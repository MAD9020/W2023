# Assignment: Checkout sequence for flower delivery

# Details

Design a checkout sequence for flower delivery.

# Requirements

Create a series of wireframes in Figma to communicate your design of a flower delivery checkout sequence, on a mobile platform of your choice (phone). Assemble and save your work as a single, multi-page pdf (or slide deck, or other format) that a reader can easily navigate and read.

# Scenario

Assume your user has selected a bouquet of flowers and is about to click the Order now button.

# Your checkout sequence must include at least the following fields:

  • Sender first name
  • Sender last name
  • Sender email address
  • Sender phone number
  • Recipient first name
  • Recipient last name
  • Recipient address
  • Recipient City
  • Recipient Province/State
  • Recipient Postal Code/Zip
  • Recipient Country
  • Recipient Phone number (1)
  • Recipient Phone number (2)
  • Delivery date
  • Delivery details
  • Message to recipient
  • Billing - First Name
  • Billing - Last Name
  • Credit card number
  • Expiry date
  • Security code
  • Billing full address (allow reusing recipient info)
  • Billing phone number
  • Billing email
  • Extras (Add balloon, chocolates, stuffed toy, gift card, etc.)

# Wireframe at least:

  1. The entry screen of the sequence (What screen is the user on when they decide to order? What do they tap?)
  2. Checkout sequence form (can be a single, long, scrolling screen, or divided up into multiple steps)
  3. Detailed views of at least 3 controls (how does it look when user taps on a field/button?)
  4. What appears when user successfully completes the form?
  5. Error handling (what appears on screen if errors are made in form completion?)

# Things to consider:

  • Does your design comply with your chosen platform's design guidelines?
  • Are your fields grouped into logical sub-groups?
  • Does your layout help users understand where to focus, where to go next, etc?
  • Did you use suitable controls for the type of information being captured?
  • Have you indicated which fields are mandatory, and which are optional?
  • Does your wireframe require any annotations to communicate the design to the person reading it?

Grading criteria (25 points)

  • Did student use appropriate controls for the type of information being entered? (5 pts)
  • Did student logically group content into sub-sections, and use layout principles (alignment, spacing, positioning, etc.) to help user understand and complete the form? (5 pts)
  • Did student include all necessary wireframes? (5 pts)
  • How well did the document/set of wireframes communicate the design intentions? (5 pts)
  • Did student attend 1:1 Feedback sessions in Week 11 & Week 12? (5 pts)
